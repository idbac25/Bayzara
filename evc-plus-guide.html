EVC Plus Merchant Integration — Complete Guide

How Hormud EVC Plus is connected to your Bayzara business account

When You Enter Your Merchant ID & Password

When you go to EVC Plus → Connect and type in your Hormud merchant username and password, here is exactly what Bayzara does behind the scenes:

1Bayzara logs into Hormud on your behalf
Your credentials are sent securely from your browser to Bayzara's server — never stored in the browser or shared with anyone. The server contacts Hormud's merchant API directly and logs in, exactly like you would on the Hormud merchant website. Hormud responds with a temporary session token (a digital key that expires after 8 hours).
2Your balance and account name are fetched immediately
Using the session token, Bayzara immediately retrieves your current EVC wallet balance and your merchant account name. This is displayed on the confirmation screen so you can verify it is the correct account before activating.
3You click Confirm — the connection goes live
Nothing is saved as "active" until you confirm. When you click Activate, two things happen automatically: (1) your EVC connection is marked as live in Bayzara's database, and (2) a new Payment Account is created inside your business under Bank Accounts, linked directly to your EVC wallet.

What Runs Automatically Every 60 Seconds

After activation, Bayzara has a background service — called a "sync engine" — that wakes up every minute and processes your EVC account automatically. Here is what it does each cycle:

1Checks for new incoming payments
It asks Hormud: "Are there any new payments since the last one we recorded?" Up to 50 new transactions are fetched per cycle. The system never imports the same transaction twice — every transaction has a unique ID (tran_id) and duplicates are automatically blocked.
2Looks up who sent each payment
For every transaction, it checks the sender's phone number against your client list. If the phone number matches a client's saved EVC Phone field, the transaction is automatically linked to that client. If no match is found, the sender's name is looked up from Hormud's customer profile API.
3Matches the payment to an open invoice
If a client is identified, Bayzara looks for their oldest unpaid or overdue invoice. If the payment amount exactly matches the amount owed on that invoice, the payment is automatically recorded and the invoice is marked as paid — with zero manual work from you.
4Flags anything uncertain for your review
If the sender is unknown, the amount doesn't match any invoice, or the client has no outstanding invoices, the transaction is saved and marked "Needs Review." It will appear in your EVC Hub where you can manually assign it to the correct client and invoice.
5Updates your live wallet balance
Your current EVC wallet balance is refreshed every minute. The latest balance is always shown on your dashboard and in the EVC Hub.

What Happens in Different Scenarios

Situation What Bayzara Does Action Needed?
Client pays exact invoice amount, phone is saved Auto-records payment, marks invoice paid ✓ None
Client phone is saved but amount differs slightly Links to client, flags for review Confirm in EVC Hub
Unknown phone number (no client match) Saves transaction, marked "Needs Review" Assign manually
Client exists but no open invoices Links to client, flags for review Assign to correct document
Duplicate transaction received Automatically ignored ✓ None

The One Thing That Makes It Work Best

💡 Key tip: The entire auto-matching system depends on one thing — having each client's EVC phone number saved in their profile. Go to Clients → [Client Name] → Edit and fill in the EVC Phone field. Once that is done, every future payment from that client is matched and recorded automatically.

Do You Need to Do Anything After Connecting?

For the most part, no — the system runs on its own. But here is a quick checklist:

One-time setup (do this once)

✅ Connect your EVC merchant account (enter username + password, confirm)
✅ Add the EVC phone number to each of your clients' profiles

Ongoing (only when needed)

🔍 Check the EVC Hub → Needs Review tab occasionally for any unmatched transactions
🔁 If your Hormud session expires (after 8 hours), you may need to reconnect once

⚠️ Important — Background sync deployment: The automatic every-60-second sync is a Supabase Edge Function that needs to be deployed separately to Supabase's servers. Until it is deployed, transactions will not import automatically. You can still trigger a manual sync from the EVC Hub at any time. Ask your developer to run supabase functions deploy evc-sync when you are ready to go fully live.

Summary

Think of it like this: Bayzara acts as your accountant who watches your EVC merchant account 24/7. Every time money comes in, it checks if it knows who sent it, finds the matching invoice, and ticks it off — all automatically. If it's not sure, it puts it in a pile and asks you to check.
